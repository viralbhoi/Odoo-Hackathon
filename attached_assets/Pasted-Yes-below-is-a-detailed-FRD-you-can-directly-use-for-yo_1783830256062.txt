FRD overview
Project Name: TransitOps – Smart Transport Operations Platform.

Purpose: Build a centralized transport operations system that replaces spreadsheets and manual logs with a role-based platform for fleet control, dispatch, compliance, maintenance tracking, operational costing, and reporting.

Hackathon context: The solution must be practical enough to demonstrate end-to-end lifecycle coverage within 8 hours, so the FRD should prioritize core workflows, clear validations, automation of status changes, and analytics that prove business value.

Business problem
Many logistics teams still manage vehicle allocation, dispatch planning, driver compliance, maintenance, and expenses manually, which causes scheduling conflicts, vehicle underutilization, missed maintenance, expired-license risk, inaccurate cost records, and poor operational visibility. TransitOps addresses this by providing one system for vehicle registration, driver records, trip execution, maintenance state control, fuel logging, and operational analytics.

Goals
Centralize transport operations data in one application.

Prevent invalid dispatches through automated business-rule validation.

Improve visibility through KPIs like active vehicles, active trips, drivers on duty, maintenance counts, and fleet utilization.

Track operating cost using fuel and maintenance data and expose financial insights such as operational cost, fuel efficiency, and vehicle ROI.

Success criteria
Users can create, view, update, and manage vehicles and drivers without data duplication or invalid states.

Dispatch only allows compliant drivers and eligible vehicles.

Status transitions happen automatically on dispatch, completion, cancellation, and maintenance events.

Dashboard and reports reflect near-real-time operational data.

Business rules

Risks and edge cases
A strong FRD should also mention edge conditions because these often become demo-breaking bugs.

Important edge cases
Dispatch attempted with a vehicle that changed to In Shop in another session.

Driver license expires today; define whether current date counts as valid until end-of-day.

Completing trip without actual distance or odometer update.

Cancelling draft vs cancelling dispatched trip should behave differently.

Retired vehicle with open maintenance.

Fuel logs entered without trip reference.

ROI unavailable when revenue is not tracked; in that case mark ROI as “Not enough data” unless revenue field is included.

Project Name: TransitOps – Smart Transport Operations Platform.

Objective: Build a secure, scalable, role-based transport operations platform that digitizes vehicle management, driver management, dispatch, maintenance, fuel logging, expense tracking, and operational analytics while enforcing business-critical validations and automated lifecycle transitions.

Chosen Stack: React + TypeScript frontend; Node.js, Express.js, and TypeScript backend; PostgreSQL with Prisma ORM; JWT and refresh token auth with Google OAuth; Redis, BullMQ, Swagger, Zod, Helmet, CORS, rate limiting, Pino, and Sentry for platform hardening.

System goals
TransitOps must solve the operational issues described in the problem statement: scheduling conflicts, underutilized vehicles, missed maintenance, expired license risk, inaccurate expense tracking, and low operational visibility. Support RBAC for Fleet Manager, Driver, Safety Officer, and Financial Analyst.

Business rule enforcement
The problem statement’s mandatory rules are the heart of the system and must be executed atomically in backend services.

Mandatory rule implementations
Vehicle registration number unique: enforce at DB unique index and service pre-check.

Retired/In Shop vehicle excluded from dispatch: dispatch query must filter and final dispatch transaction must recheck status.

Expired/Suspended driver cannot be assigned: validate against current date and status.

On Trip resource cannot be reused: query and transaction recheck must both exist.

Cargo weight cannot exceed capacity: validate against selected vehicle capacity.

Dispatch updates both driver and vehicle to On Trip in one transaction.

Trip completion returns both to Available in one transaction.

Trip cancellation restores both to Available if previously dispatched.

Opening maintenance sets vehicle to In Shop.

Closing maintenance restores vehicle to Available unless Retired.

Transaction design
Use prisma.$transaction() for:

dispatch trip

cancel trip

open maintenance

close maintenance

This prevents partial state updates such as a trip moving to Dispatched while the vehicle remains Available.
